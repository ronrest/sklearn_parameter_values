Effect of Modifying Individual Parameters for kNN¶

This document looks at the effect that modifying the values of individial parameters to the k-Nearest Neighbors algorithm in sklearn has on its accuracy and time taken to train the model.

Best results were when we increased the sample size to 4000:

sample_size = 4000
n_neighbors = 8        # k
weights = "uniform"  
algorithm = "auto"

Giving an out of sample accuracy of 0.963

If we were to keep the sample size at the default value of 1000, then the greatest results were obtained when the following settings were used:

sample_size = 1000
n_neighbors = 8        # k          
weights = "uniform"  
algorithm = "auto"

Giving an out of sample accuracy of 0.944
